What is

Pinterest?

Pinterest is a tool for collecting and organizing things you love

Pinterest Is

A virtual pinboard site that lets you "pin" or collect images from the web

Terms

  • pin: to "stick" something to your board
  • re-pin: to pin something someone else has already pinned
  • pinner: someone who pins things
  • board: a collection of pins organized by topic
  • pin it button: a webpage button that enables pinning
  • pin bookmarklet: a browser toolbar button for pinning
  • like: a way of approving another pinners pin
  • comment: something that a pinner says about a pin

Whatever the interest, people tend to pursue them through four stages:

Aspiration

the moment you get inspired to pursue a specific interest

(say, taking up golf)

Anticipation

the phase where people look into the interest and start prepping to actually do it

(like, researching golf clubs)

Participation

(this is the fun part!)

when people actually participate in the interest they’ve decided to pursue (like, playing nine holes at a local course)

Reflection

where people reflect on the activity, maybe by taking a photo and framing it or sharing it on a social network

Pinterest is most often used in the

first three stages

The most successful businesses on our service help people along the journey.

It’s about identifying how your business fits in with a person’s interests and becoming part of how they pursue and participate in that interest.

To use Pinterest successfully

think about the interests your business shares with

customers

  • For a food company, it might be cooking
  • For a travel company, it might be vacation planning
  • For an outdoor goods retailer, it’s likely helping people discover and go on great hikes

You can get started on Pinterest for free, but if you really want to succeed you will need to

invest in content

Successful Pinterest users update consistently, so you’ll need to make sure you have enough

visual content available

and people in place to update your boards regularly.

Visual imagery drives

inspiration

It’s what makes you want to buy things
